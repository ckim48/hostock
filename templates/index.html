Technical Indicators and Benchmark

This website offers information on five technical indicators: Stochastic Relative Strength Index (RSI), Moving Average Convergence/Divergence (MACD), MACD Breakout, Breakout, and Bollinger-Band. Each indicator is briefly explained below, along with details on the Benchmark.

  • Stochastic Relative Strength Index (RSI)

    The Stochastic Relative Strength Index (RSI) is a technical analysis indicator that combines elements of the Stochastic Oscillator and the traditional Relative Strength Index. It provides a measure of overbought or oversold conditions in a market by considering both price momentum and RSI values. The Stochastic RSI generates signals based on the crossing of predefined levels, helping traders identify potential entry or exit points.

  • Moving Average Convergence Divergence (MACD)

    The Moving Average Convergence Divergence (MACD) is a widely used technical indicator that helps traders identify potential changes in trend direction and generate buy or sell signals. It is calculated by subtracting the longer-term exponential moving average (EMA) from the shorter-term EMA. The MACD line crossing above or below the signal line is often considered a bullish or bearish signal, respectively.

  • MACD Breakout

    The MACD Breakout strategy is a trading strategy that combines the MACD and the Breakout algorithms created for virtual simulations. It purports to address the weaknesses of the MACD and Breakout by combining the two together.

  • Breakout

    The Volatility Breakout (Breakout) strategy is employed by traders to take advantage of significant price movements that follow a period of low volatility. By identifying instances where the market price breaks out of a consolidation phase, indicating increased volatility, traders can use this breakout as a signal to enter a trade and anticipate a continuation of the price movement.

  • Bollinger-Bands

    Bollinger Bands serve as a technical analysis tool for measuring volatility in financial markets. It consists of an upper and lower band that represent standard deviations from the middle band. The strategy is commonly used by traders to identify potential price reversals and evaluate whether an asset is overbought or oversold.

  • Benchmark

    The benchmark strategy, also known as the buy-and-hold strategy, is an investing technique in which an investor buys a stock and holds it for the long term, even if the stock price fluctuates in the short term. This strategy is based on the idea that over time, the stock market will trend upwards, so investors who buy and hold stocks will eventually see their investment grow in value.

Using the Natural Language Tool Kit (NLTK), this website first tokenizes words included in news article titles collected from Google News. This process separates all words from news articles. Then, the website removes stop words. The stop words are negligible words that do not convey any sentiment, such as 'is' and 'a. After removing stop words, the analysis proceeds to lemmatization. This process combines words with the same roots. It ensures that the sentiment analyzer does not process overlapping meanings in the next step. Next, this website uses the Sentiment Intensity Analyzer from NLTK to assign news titles polarity scores between -1 and 1. Finally, based on the polarity scores, news title sentiments are either regarded as negative, neutral, or positive.

News title sentiments are important to technical analysis as they help point out different sentiment contexts for information, which influence technical analysis performances.
